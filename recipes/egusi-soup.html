EGUSI SOUP RECIPE

Description

Egusi soup is a hearty Nigerian classic made from ground melon seeds, cooked down into a thick, richly flavored stew with palm oil, leafy greens like spinach or ugu, and a generous mix of proteins such as beef, goat meat, or fish. The egusi seeds give the soup its signature texture(slightly grainy and satisfying) while ingredients like onions, peppers, and stock cubes build a deep, savory base. It's traditionally slow-cooked to let all the flavors meld together, and it's almost always served with a swallow like pounded yam, eba, or fufu, making it a filling, comforting meal that's a staple at Nigerian tables everywhere.

Ingredients

  • Three quarters cup egusi seeds or pumpkin seeds
  • One and a half pounds cubed beef stew meat
  • Salt to taste
  • Half cup of peanut oil
  • Two large tomatoes, chopped
  • One small onions, chopped
  • Two habanero peppers, seeded and minced
  • Two pounds frsh shrimp, peeled and devined
  • Eighteen ounces tomato sauce
  • One and a half cups of water
  • Three tablespoons of tomato paste
  • One pound of fresh spinach, washed and chopped

Steps

  1. Place seeds in a blender; blend until mixture is powdery, 30 to 40 seconds. Set aside

  2. Cut beef into bite-sized cubes; season with salt.

  3. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Cook beef in hot oil until brown but not cooked through, 3 to 5 minutes.

  4. Place tomatoes, onion, and peppers in a blender; blend until smooth, about 30 seconds. Stir tomato mixture into beef; reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Cook until meat is tender, 40 to 50 minutes.

  5. Add shrimp, tomato sauce, water, and tomato paste; simmer for 10 minutes.

  6. Stir in spinach and powdered seeds. Continue simmering for 10 more minutes.

NOTE
If you can't find egusi seeds, you can substitute pumpkin seeds.Any combination of crab, shrimp, and smoked fish can be used in place of the shrimp.Drained, smoked oysters and chicken can be used in place of the beef.
