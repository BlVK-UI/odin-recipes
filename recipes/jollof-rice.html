JOLLOF RICE RECIPE

Description

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made by cooking rice in a rich, smoky tomato and pepper base, simmered slowly with onions, garlic, and a blend of warm spices until every grain soaks up the flavor. It's often cooked with chicken or beef stock for extra depth, and finished with a touch of butter or oil for that signature glossy finish. Whether served at parties, family dinners, or just a regular weeknight meal, jollof rice is known for its bold, slightly spicy taste and the unmistakable aroma that fills the kitchen while it cooks.

Ingredients

  • One tablespoon olive oil
  • One large onion, sliced
  • Two (14.5 ounce) cans stewed tomatoes
  • Half (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • One teaspoon salt
  • Quarter teaspoon black pepper
  • Quarter teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Half teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • One tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • One teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • Two cups water
  • One (3 pound) whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • One cup uncooked white rice
  • One cup diced carrots
  • Half pound fresh green beans, trimmed and snapped into 1 to 2 inch pieces
  • Quarter teaspoon ground nutmeg

Steps

  1. Pour oil into large saucepan. Cook onion in oil over medium-low heat until translucent.

  2. Stir in stewed tomatoes and tomato paste, and season with salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, red pepper flakes, Worcestershire sauce and rosemary. Cover, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, stir in water, and add chicken pieces. Simmer for 30 minutes.

  3. Stir in rice, carrots, and green beans, and season with nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover, and simmer until the chicken is fork-tender and the rice is cooked, 25 to 30 minutes.
